# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo content creators and travel influencers managing hotel brand partnerships. They work alone or as a micro-business, juggling outreach, follow-ups, and relationship tracking across many properties. They open StayFind when they're in "work mode" — not casually browsing, but actively trying to move deals forward. Time is limited; cognitive overhead is the enemy.

## Product Purpose

StayFind is a hotel outreach platform: find hotels via Google Places, build targeted lists, send personalised email sequences, and track replies — all from one place. Success is a booked collab stay. Every feature exists to shorten the distance between "found this hotel" and "confirmed partnership."

## Brand Personality

Warm · Trusted · Premium. StayFind feels like a senior creative partner who happens to be a great organiser — calm, confident, never flustered. It doesn't shout. It shows up, does the work, and makes the creator look professional.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS** (HubSpot, Salesforce): bland blue enterprise energy, dense forms, zero soul
- **Influencer-pink** (Flodesk, Later): pastel softness, over-designed, optimised for aesthetics over utility
- **Consumer travel** (Booking.com, Airbnb): booking-engine UI — consumer-facing, not B2B outreach
- **Dark developer tool** (VS Code aesthetic, terminal green): too technical, wrong emotional register

## Design Principles

1. **Warm confidence** — approachable and high-quality without being precious. The tool feels like it costs more than it does.
2. **Progress is the product** — every screen should show the creator they're one step closer to a booked stay. Pipeline momentum is the emotional reward.
3. **Earned restraint** — detail and quality show through typography, spacing, and precision — not decoration. Nothing is there just to look good.
4. **Editorial calm** — quiet hierarchy, good type, generous whitespace (Notion-influenced). The creator's content and hotel data are the hero; chrome recedes.
5. **Tool that disappears** — interaction is fast, flows are obvious, the interface never makes you think about itself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Core workflow (search → list → sequence) must be keyboard-navigable. Contrast checked on all text/background combinations, including the warm off-white body. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`.
